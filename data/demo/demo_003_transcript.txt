DEMO CALL TRANSCRIPT — AlarmTech Security Systems
Date: 2024-11-15  |  Duration: 31 min  |  Participants: Marcus Webb (Clara Sales), Sandra Reeves (AlarmTech Director of Operations)

---

MARCUS: Sandra, let's get started. What's the pain you're trying to solve?

SANDRA: We're a commercial alarm company based in Atlanta. We install and monitor fire and burglar alarms for warehouses, retail, office buildings. We have about 400 monitored accounts. Our call volume is high — 60 to 80 calls a day. A lot of those are just customers asking basic questions or wanting to test their systems. We need help triaging.

MARCUS: So it's less about after-hours emergencies and more about volume management?

SANDRA: Both, honestly. During business hours, our front desk is overwhelmed. After hours, we have a monitoring center that handles alarms, but customers call our main line wanting to speak to someone. We need Clara to handle that first contact.

MARCUS: Let's talk about business hours first. What are they?

SANDRA: Monday through Friday, 8am to 5:30pm. Eastern time. We're in Buckhead, Atlanta.

MARCUS: And what do those daytime calls look like?

SANDRA: Customers asking about their invoice, requesting a service call for a faulty sensor, scheduling annual inspections, false alarm calls — someone accidentally set it off and just wants to talk to someone.

MARCUS: And after hours?

SANDRA: Anything that's a live alarm situation — like they're getting calls from their monitoring center and they want to reach a real person. Or a sensor malfunctioned and they need someone tonight. Also break-ins, obviously.

MARCUS: Do you dispatch techs directly or route to a monitoring center?

SANDRA: We have an on-call tech rotation. At night it routes to our monitoring coordinator first, then to the on-call tech. The monitoring center is separate and handles the alarm signals — Clara would only handle calls to our main business line.

MARCUS: Good distinction. What would be a non-emergency in your world?

SANDRA: Low battery warning on a panel, a sensor offline but no alarm triggered, customer wants to add a new zone to their system. Those can wait.

MARCUS: Any field service software?

SANDRA: We use Alarm.com and also have our own CRM, ServiceMax. We'd need Clara to never promise to create tickets directly — our team does that.

MARCUS: Noted. Let me show you the demo.

[Demo interaction — 14 minutes]

SANDRA: I like the tone. Can it handle situations where a customer is upset or panicking?

MARCUS: We design it to be calm and reassuring — if the caller seems distressed, it shortens its prompts and focuses on getting information quickly.

SANDRA: That's important for us. What about if the caller hangs up during transfer?

MARCUS: Clara logs all caller details before initiating transfer, so even if the call drops, your team has everything they need to call back.

SANDRA: Okay. I think we want to move forward.
